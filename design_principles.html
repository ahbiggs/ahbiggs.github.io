Fitt's Law

YouTube

  • YouTube.com

Fitt's Law is a calculation that predicts the time it takes for a user to find an action. For example in this image, you can see that due to the size, placement, and color of the 'sign-in' button, it would not take the user long to locate it and to use it.

White Space

Apple

  • Apple.com

White space is a design method used to help display a design in the simplest way possible, in order to put a larger emphasis on the product being displayed.

Visual Hierarchy

Madewell

  • Madewell.com

Visual Hierarchy is used in design to emphasize an element based on size, shape, or color, in order to draw attention to it. This example shows a screenshot of a clothing website. The black banner at the top offers a strong contrast to the rest of the elements, so the user sees it first.
