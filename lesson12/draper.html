History of Draper, Utah

Draper was established in 1849 by Ebenezer and Phoebe Brown. They were joined by other settlers including William and Elizabeth Draper. Originally, the town was called "Brownsville" but was later changed to "Draperville", named after the first bishop in the area, William Draper. The population has grown dramatically from just around 5,521 in 1980 to over 47,000 today!
